The prompt: Create a six word auto-biography and display the quote in some typographic way.

I've always had self-esteem issues, and I've worried, in varying degrees throughout my life, about how people judge me. I'm also participant to the very large community of judges around the world. Everyone has something to judge and scorn, and everyone has flaws in the eyes of everyone else. If there was a population of individuals who are truly perfect, maybe being judged for certain characteristics or actions is something to worry about. But no one is perfect. So even if my best friends judge me for something I do or something I have, well it certainly doesn't matter, because if it was that significant to them, we wouldn't be friends.

The cacophony of yarn creates of visual noise representative of that internal struggle someone goes through when they change their hairstyle or their life choices: "What will they think? Does it look okay? Will I look artificial? Superficial?"

Crack'd was installed in Gallery 5 at the Boston University College of Fine Arts. Tanner Gauvin (BU CFA'18) and I explored the effects of visual fragmentation with an exhibition showcasing fragmented imagery, geometric forms, and the physical disruption of space using string and mirrors.
I installed a large geometric piece using black vinyl that extended from a moveable wall to a separate wall of the gallery. From a certain point of view, the two walls merged into one continuous composition, while from other angles, the vinyl separated into two disconnected walls.
Situated between the two walls of vinyl was a corner in which hung a web of string that created shadows on the walls. Viewers were encouraged to enter the space and sit down. As the only enclosed space in the gallery, it was a space that allowed the viewer to remove him/herself from others, while simultaneously becoming a semi-omniscient voyeur—the many mirrors provided views throughout the gallery in unexpected places.
